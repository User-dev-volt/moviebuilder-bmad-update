# Lighting Reference

Reference data for the Visual Architect during lighting protocol creation.

## Common Lighting Styles

| Style | Characteristics | Mood | Film References |
|-------|----------------|------|-----------------|
| Chiaroscuro | Deep shadows, strong key light, minimal fill, 60%+ shadow coverage | Dramatic, mysterious, powerful | The Godfather, Sin City |
| High-Key | Even, bright lighting, minimal shadows, low contrast | Cheerful, clean, open, optimistic | The Truman Show, Her |
| Neon-Noir | Colored rim lights, deep blacks, saturated neon accents, volumetric haze | Urban, dystopian, electric, dangerous | Blade Runner 2049, Drive |
| Naturalistic | Motivated sources only, soft daylight, practical lights | Authentic, grounded, intimate | The Revenant, Moonlight |
| Expressionist | Extreme angles, harsh shadows, distorted light shapes | Anxiety, psychological tension, unease | The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari, Se7en |
| Low-Key | Predominantly dark, single strong source, deep shadows | Tension, intimacy, secrecy | Zodiac, No Country for Old Men |
| Rembrandt | Triangle of light on shadow-side cheek, classic portrait lighting | Dignified, classical, timeless | Girl with a Pearl Earring |
| Silhouette | Subject backlit, no fill, figure defined by outline only | Mystery, anonymity, power | The Third Man |

## Light Source Types

| Source Type | Purpose | Typical Position |
|-------------|---------|------------------|
| Key Light | Primary illumination, defines subject form | 45° to subject, slightly above |
| Fill Light | Reduces shadow contrast from key | Opposite side of key, lower intensity |
| Rim/Back Light | Separates subject from background, defines edges | Behind subject |
| Ambient/Practical | Motivates the scene's light source (lamp, window, screen) | Within scene |
| Accent/Kicker | Draws attention to specific element | Variable |

## Lighting Rules Examples

Common enforceable rules used in productions:
- "NEVER use flat, even lighting"
- "ALWAYS include volumetric atmosphere (haze, fog, dust)"
- "Rim light must define subject edges in every shot"
- "Shadows should occupy 60%+ of frame area"
- "Practical light sources must be visible or implied in every interior"
- "No unmotivated light — every source must have a diegetic reason"
- "Key-to-fill ratio must be at least 4:1"
- "Color temperature shifts between interior (warm) and exterior (cool)"
