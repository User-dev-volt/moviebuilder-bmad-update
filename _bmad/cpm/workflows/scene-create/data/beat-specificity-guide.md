# Beat Specificity Guide

The beats section is the creative core of scene-create. Each beat becomes one shard in shard-generation — a 5-8 second clip the Showrunner must interpret. The more specific the beat, the better the Showrunner can direct the Prompt Engineer.

---

## The Specificity Test

Ask yourself: **Could a director call "action" on this beat?**

A good beat describes a physical action so precisely that there is only one way to film it.

---

## Vague vs. Specific: Examples

### ❌ VAGUE — Too interpretive, multiple filming options
- "They have a tense moment"
- "Elias reacts emotionally"
- "Mara plays the piano"
- "The characters connect"
- "He remembers something"

### ✅ SPECIFIC — One filming option, camera knows what to do
- "Elias's RIGHT hand closes the checkbook. He does not look at Mara."
- "Elias's jaw tightens. His eyes drop to the receipt on the table, then return to Mara's face."
- "Mara's LEFT hand finds the opening chord — a single note, held. Her eyes stay closed."
- "Mara sets her tea cup down with both hands. The porcelain barely makes a sound."
- "Elias's RIGHT hand moves toward the door handle, stops two inches short."

---

## The Guiding Questions

When a beat feels vague, ask the filmmaker:

**For action:**
- "What does [character]'s hand DO?"
- "What does their body DO — not what they feel, what they physically do?"
- "Where does the camera look?"

**For duration check:**
- "Is this one action or several? If several, split it."
- "Could this happen in 5 seconds? 8 seconds? If it needs more, split it."

**For focus:**
- "Who does the camera follow in this beat?"
- "What is the single most important thing the audience sees?"

---

## 5-8 Second Calibration

**5 seconds** = one contained action
> "Elias picks up the receipt and reads it."

**8 seconds** = one action with a reaction
> "Elias picks up the receipt, reads it, and sets it back down without expression."

**Too long (split this):**
> "Elias reads the receipt, processes its meaning, looks at Mara, then walks to the window."
→ Split into: receipt reading (shard N) + turning to Mara (shard N+1) + walking to window (shard N+2)

---

## What Belongs in Each Beat Field

**Action:** The physical event. Verb-first. Body part specified where relevant. What the camera captures.
> "Mara's LEFT hand reaches for Elias's arm, stops before contact."

**Character Focus:** Who the camera emphasizes. Can be both characters if interaction.
> "Mara" / "Elias" / "Mara and Elias" / "Mara's hand"

**Emotional Note:** The feeling of the moment — not what the character feels internally, but the emotional texture the audience experiences.
> "The gap between them is the whole scene." / "Quiet certainty." / "The moment before something breaks."
