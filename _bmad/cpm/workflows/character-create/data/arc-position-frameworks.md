# Arc Position Frameworks

**Purpose:** Reference frameworks for character transformation arcs - emotional states, want vs. need, arc progress.

---

## Arc Progress Scale (0-100%)

### 0-25%: Beginning (Denial)
- **Emotional State:** Confident in false belief, unaware of need
- **Behavior:** Pursues want without questioning
- **Example:** Detective believes solving cases = success, doesn't see they're avoiding personal relationships

### 26-50%: Awareness (Recognition)
- **Emotional State:** Starting to see cracks in belief, resistant to change
- **Behavior:** Moments of doubt, still clinging to want
- **Example:** Detective realizes loneliness, but doubles down on work

### 51-75%: Struggle (Resistance)
- **Emotional State:** Torn between want and need, fear of change
- **Behavior:** Trying both paths, failing at commitment
- **Example:** Detective attempts relationship but sabotages it with work

### 76-100%: Transformation (Acceptance)
- **Emotional State:** Embracing need, letting go of want
- **Behavior:** Changed behavior aligned with need
- **Example:** Detective chooses relationship over case, finds balance

---

## Want vs. Need Examples

### Example 1: Revenge Arc

**Want:** Kill the person who murdered their family
**Need:** Learn to forgive and heal

**Arc Journey:**
- 0%: Consumed by revenge, believes killing will bring peace
- 50%: Realizes revenge won't bring family back, still can't let go
- 100%: Chooses mercy, finds peace in forgiveness

### Example 2: Prove Yourself Arc

**Want:** Prove they're the best (detective, warrior, leader)
**Need:** Accept they don't have to be perfect to be worthy

**Arc Journey:**
- 0%: Takes dangerous risks to prove superiority
- 50%: Failure teaches humility, resists lesson
- 100%: Accepts limitations, finds strength in team

### Example 3: Control Arc

**Want:** Control everything to feel safe
**Need:** Trust others and accept uncertainty

**Arc Journey:**
- 0%: Micromanages, pushes people away
- 50%: Control backfires, causes more chaos
- 100%: Lets go, trusts partners, finds peace

### Example 4: Redemption Arc

**Want:** Escape past mistakes
**Need:** Face past and make amends

**Arc Journey:**
- 0%: Running, hiding identity
- 50%: Past catches up, forced to confront
- 100%: Owns mistakes, makes things right

## Emotional States by Arc Position

### Early Arc (0-25%)
- Confident
- Determined
- Naive
- Stubborn
- Defensive

### Mid Arc (26-75%)
- Conflicted
- Frustrated
- Desperate
- Doubtful
- Vulnerable

### Late Arc (76-100%)
- Accepting
- Resolved
- At peace
- Transformed
- Humbled

## Common Want/Need Patterns

| Want | Need |
|------|------|
| Revenge | Forgiveness |
| Prove superiority | Accept worth isn't performance |
| Control everything | Trust and let go |
| Escape past | Face and heal |
| Be loved | Love self first |
| Win at all costs | Define success differently |
| Be right | Be happy |
| Safety through isolation | Connection despite risk |

---

## Using Arc Position in Production

**Showrunner checks:**
- Is character action motivated by want or need?
- Does arc progress match story point?
- Is transformation earned, not sudden?

**Script Supervisor verifies:**
- Character behavior aligns with arc position
- Emotional state matches progress %
- Want/need conflict is visible in decisions

**Example Scene Check (Arc = 45%):**
- ❌ Character suddenly chooses need (too early)
- ✅ Character struggles between want and need (appropriate for 45%)
- ❌ Character fully committed to want (regressing)
