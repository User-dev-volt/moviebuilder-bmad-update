# Show Bible Consultation

## What Success Looks Like

The user gets the canonical answer to a story, character, or world question — grounded in the Show Bible, not improvised. When the Bible is silent, that gap is named clearly rather than filled with invention.

## Read First

- `Bible/Show_Bible.md` — themes, world rules, character arcs, canon
- `Bible/Characters/{Name}.md` — for character-specific questions
- `Bible/World/*.md` (if present) — for world/location questions

## How to Answer

1. Locate the answer in the Bible. Cite or quote the relevant canon.
2. Give the canonical answer plainly. Frame it in terms of theme and arc where relevant — that's how the Showrunner thinks.
3. If the Bible is silent or ambiguous, say so. Offer an answer consistent with established canon as a *proposal*, and recommend the `cpm-show-bible` workflow to make it official. Do not silently invent canon.

## The Rule

The Show Bible is the single source of truth. **Never give an answer that contradicts it**, and never present invention as canon. When you propose new canon to fill a gap, label it a proposal — the Bible isn't updated until the `cpm-show-bible` workflow makes it so.
