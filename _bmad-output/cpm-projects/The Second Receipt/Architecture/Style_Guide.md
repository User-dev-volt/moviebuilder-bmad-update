# Cinematic Style Guide — The Second Receipt

## Visual Identity Statement

*The Second Receipt* is filmed in **Thermal Realism** — a world split by a single vertical law: Cold geometry belongs to the System; warm texture belongs to the Soul. Every frame is a thermometer. Elias begins the film boxed inside rectangles — car windows, tablet borders, door frames — a man whose life has been edited into right angles. As Mara's world works on him, the frame's geometry begins to fail: curves invade, dust fills the air, and the piano's amber wood refuses to stay in the background. When music plays, the camera stops entirely — stillness as a physical law, honoring the Sonic Priority rule that in this world, sound moves faster than light. The visual journey is not a plot; it is a slow thermal thaw — a cold blue winter morning being swallowed, frame by frame, by a deep amber sunset.

## Lighting Protocol

**Primary Style: The Siege Protocol**
*Motivated Binary Chiaroscuro — two philosophically opposed lighting regimes, hard border between them, no blending.*

**Characteristics:**
1. Cold Mode — single source, hard-cut shadows, zero fill, sterile void
2. Warm Mode — motivated practicals, natural volume (day) or retreating concentration (night)
3. Border State — threshold objects lit by both sources meeting, no dedicated key
4. Night = siege — cold system light presses inward, warm world retreats and concentrates

| Source | Color Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cold Key | Tablet Blue | `#5B8DD9` | Elias uplight — tablet glow from below, chin/chest |
| System Ambient | System Green | `#8FBCAA` | Hallways, stairwells, street — fluorescent siege |
| Warm Key (Day) | Legacy Gold | `#F0C04A` | Golden-hour window — Mara's apartment, daylight |
| Warm Key (Night) | Tungsten Retreat | `#D4722A` | Piano lamp, floor lamp — warmth under siege |
| Border Accent | Receipt Edge | *(no own hex)* | Receipt, doorways — lit by meeting of both sources |

**Lighting Rules:**
- [ ] NEVER feather shadow edges in Cold spaces — all cut-offs are hard lines, zero gradient
- [ ] NEVER use fill light in Cold spaces — the shadow is void, not atmosphere
- [ ] ALWAYS use motivated practical sources — every light must have a visible or implied diegetic reason
- [ ] Cold light and Warm light may touch a border object but must NEVER blend or mix in the same space
- [ ] Night interior: Warm world retreats to practical tungsten only — no broad ambient fill survives
- [ ] Border objects (receipt, threshold spaces) are lit by the meeting of both sources — no dedicated key

## Color Palette

**Allowed Colors:**

| Name | Hex | Usage Context |
|---|---|---|
| Powered-Down Screen | `#0B101A` | All shadows in Cold spaces — sterile void, not mystery |
| Slate Blue | `#2C3E50` | Elias's suit, car, corporate architecture — visual armor, oppressive weight |
| Tablet Blue | `#5B8DD9` | Tablet screen uplight — Elias's cold key source |
| System Green | `#8FBCAA` | Hallway/stairwell fluorescents — the siege light |
| Institutional Bleach | `#F0F4F8` | Legal documents, sterile walls — The Erasure |
| Organic Parchment | `#EDE0C4` | Mara's "whites" — aged lace, piano keys, sheet music, tea saucers |
| Piano Mahogany | `#7B3F2A` | Piano wood, aged wood surfaces — the immovable anchor |
| Tungsten Retreat | `#D4722A` | Practical lamp light — warmth under siege, night scenes |
| Legacy Gold | `#F0C04A` | Golden-hour window — Mara's world in daylight |

**Forbidden Colors:**

| Color | Reason |
|---|---|
| Saturated Crimson (e.g. `#E60000`) | Signals thriller urgency and physical violence — this film moves in slow erosion, not confrontation |
| Pure Black (`#000000`) | Too cinematic and mysterious — Cold shadows belong to the System, not the romantic void |
| Pure White (`#FFFFFF`) | Too clean and heavenly — The Erasure is institutional, not divine |
| Neon / High-Vis Synthetics (e.g. highlighter yellow, electric magenta) | Introduces cyberpunk/pop frequency — light in this film glows or radiates, it never screams from a pigment |
| Easter Pastels (e.g. mint green, baby pink, powder blue) | Communicates weightlessness — this film is about weight, and pastels would undercut the melancholic tension |

**Color Meanings:**

- **`#0B101A` Powered-Down Screen:** The System at rest — not mystery, not depth, just absence of processing. The dark that doesn't breathe.
- **`#2C3E50` Slate Blue:** Visual armor — a storm cloud that never breaks, all weight, no release. The color of authority that has forgotten it was once chosen by a person.
- **`#5B8DD9` Tablet Blue:** The language of data — cold arithmetic translated into light, the color that turns Mara into a case number.
- **`#8FBCAA` System Green:** The face of infrastructure — the color that makes skin look tired and eyes look empty.
- **`#F0F4F8` Institutional Bleach:** The Erasure — the color of the machinery that legally deletes a life. Violent when placed next to warmth.
- **`#EDE0C4` Organic Parchment:** Time made visible — a white that has been lived in, breathed on, and slowly loved into cream.
- **`#7B3F2A` Piano Mahogany:** The weight of legacy — the immovable anchor, the color of things made to last beyond a single owner.
- **`#D4722A` Tungsten Retreat:** Warmth under siege — the same soul as Legacy Gold, but concentrated, precious, fighting for its corner.
- **`#F0C04A` Legacy Gold:** The soul in full bloom — the color of things grown or made by hand, the world as it was before the contract arrived.

## Camera Language

**Lens Vocabulary:**

| Shot Type | Lens | Emotional Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Wide / Split Frame | 35mm | Human proportions preserved — deep focus holds both worlds without distortion |
| System Close-Up (Elias) | 85mm | Clinical compression — subject pinned against sterile architecture, surveilled |
| Intimate Close-Up (Mara) | 50mm | Natural presence — the lens disappears; we are sitting across the table, not surveilling |
| Detail / ECU (hands, receipt, keys) | 100mm | Tactile truth — obliterates background, forces the eye to reckon with physical texture |
| City Exterior / Establishing | 24mm | The city as indifferent context — characters made small by architecture |

**Shot Progressions:**

- **The Asset Scan** — Elias in his cold world: 85mm → 100mm → 85mm *(surveillance rotates around him at clinical distance — he is a data point being processed)*
- **The Mechanical Thaw** — Elias's arc across the film: System dolly 85mm → weighted handheld 50mm → Sonic Priority static *(from watched-from-outside to present-in-the-room to painting)*
- **The Siege Push** — Night interior: 35mm *(the apartment)* → 50mm slow push *(warmth concentrating, pulling toward the lamp)* → static *(the light holds its ground)*
- **The Receipt Beat** — Climactic close-up sequence: 35mm *(split frame established)* → 100mm *(hands, paper, ink — the weight of the object)* → 35mm *(release, context restored)*

**Camera Movement Rules:**

- **Default (Act I):** Heavy rails dolly only — mathematically precise starts and stops, no breath, no human pulse
- **Default (Act II/III):** Barely perceptible weighted handheld drift — breathes, has a slow warm pulse, pushes in subtly on emotional moments
- **Sonic Priority:** Absolute static — camera becomes a painting, zero movement of any kind
- **FORBIDDEN:** Whip pans *(violent energy — wrong world)*; zoom lens *(wrong kind of surveillance — this camera observes, it does not hunt)*; shaky or documentary handheld *(the thaw camera breathes, it does not shake)*; crane/jib god's-eye *(no omniscient observer — the camera is either the System or a thawing human)*

## Spatial Rules

**Axis of Action:**

- Cold world = screen-LEFT. Warm world = screen-RIGHT. Enforced absolutely across all shards.
- Elias remains screen-left even when physically inside Mara's apartment — he is an invader bringing the cold with him, incapable of occupying Warm-right until he earns it
- The 180-degree line may be crossed **exactly once**: the moment his pen contacts the checkbook — the moral and visual climax of the film
- No other dramatic motivation — not emotional intensity, not geography, not staging — permits crossing the axis

**Composition:**

- **Elias (Act I):** Dead-center, Kubrickian symmetry — the System's total order, the comfort of the perfectly controlled agent
- **Elias (Act II/III, inside apartment):** Heavy short-side — pushed to the extreme edge of the frame — the rectangles that once gave him order become a visual trap; no comfortable center to retreat to
- **Mara:** Rule of thirds throughout — generous headroom and lead room, subjects given space to exist with dignity; the camera never crowds her
- **The Piano:** Always granted compositional weight — never cropped, never pushed to edge — the immovable anchor treated as a co-subject, not a prop

**Negative Space:**

- **Elias's world:** Literal vacuum — sterile dead void that isolates him as a solitary data point on a blank graph. The empty space has no temperature, no history, no breath.
- **Mara's world:** True negative space does not exist. What appears empty is actively occupied — suspended dust motes, amber light spill, acoustic volume. Her air has weight and history.
- **The governing rule:** Elias's frame breathes nothing. Mara's frame breathes time.
